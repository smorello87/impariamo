Common Grammar Rules

Noun-Adjective Agreement

In Italian, adjectives must agree in gender and number with the nouns they modify. For example: "un libro interessante" (masculine singular) but "una storia interessante" (feminine singular).

Verb Conjugation

Italian verbs conjugate according to person, number, tense, and mood. There are three conjugation groups (-are, -ere, -ire) and many irregular verbs that must be memorized.

Definite Articles

Italian has several forms of the definite article ("the"): il, lo, la, i, gli, le. The choice depends on gender, number, and the initial letter of the following word.
